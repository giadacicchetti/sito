the sun beyond your eyes

2019


You see nothing
at the same time you look

 When a ray of light bump our eye, the sensitive cells light up and in a micro-fraction of second, we see.
There we’re living with a delay, a very small one, but real.
For me this is memory.
Reminding something is a procedure of selection: between all the days, hours and minutes we’ve lived, we remember just some. Furthermore we don’t take those moments as they are, to fix them in our minds, but we alter them. We decide ourselves what we want to remember from that moment and how to remember it, what to associate it with... Thus what remains of “what it was”?

The sun beyond your eyes is a work about memory, its visual nature and its relation to reality.
The work tries to reproduce the narrative structure of memory, researching in that the earliest visual narration to humankind.
The very first ancestor of photography.

To make this association between memory and images I used photography in its most essential form: impressed light.
Through the use of light infiltrations and damaged films with a grungy old camera 6x6, I recreated an imaginary world where the light both reveals and covers the shots, as memory depicts our mind but also changes reality and creates an illusionary world.
The light and the films become subject themselves and trace the narrative thread of the work.
